Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our notary services.

A notary public can notarize a wide variety of documents, including but not limited to:

  • Real estate documents (deeds, mortgages, affidavits)
  • Power of attorney documents
  • Loan documents
  • Affidavits and sworn statements
  • Medical directives and living wills
  • Vehicle title transfers
  • Business agreements

If you're unsure whether your document requires notarization, please contact us for guidance.

All signers must present valid, government-issued photo identification. Acceptable forms of ID include:

  • Driver's license or state ID card
  • U.S. passport or passport card
  • Military ID
  • Permanent resident card (Green Card)

The ID must be current (not expired) and contain a photograph, physical description, signature, and an identifying number.

Yes, for most notarizations, you must sign the document in the physical presence of the notary public. This is a fundamental requirement of notarization.

For acknowledgments, you may have signed the document previously, but you must appear before the notary to acknowledge that the signature is yours and that you signed willingly.

For jurats (sworn statements), you must sign the document in the notary's presence after taking an oath or affirmation.

Yes, we can notarize documents in languages other than English, but there are some important considerations:

  • The notary must be able to communicate directly with the signer to verify their understanding of the document
